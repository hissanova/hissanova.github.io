Binomial Distribution

This page is a brief summary of the binomial distribution. The mean and variance are derived without using the moment generating function.

Definition

Definition: Binomial distribution
A random variable \(X\) is said to follow the binomial distribution with parameters \(p \in (0,1)\) and \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), denoted \(X \sim Binom(n,p)\), if

  1. the sample space is given by: \(\mathcal{X} = \{0, \ldots ,n\}\), and
  2. the probability mass function is given by: \(\displaystyle f(x;n,p) = \left( n \atop p\right) p^{x}(1-p)^{n-x}\).

Properties

  • The mean: \(\mathbb{E}[X] = np\)
  • The variance: \(\text{Var}[X] = np(1-p)\)
  • The moment generating function: \(M(t) = (1- p + pe^{t})^{n}\)

Derivations of Properties

The mean

\begin{eqnarray} \mathbb{E}[X] & = & \sum_{x=0}^{n} x \left( n \atop x \right)p^{x}(1-p)^{n-x}\\ & = & \sum_{x=1}^{n} x \left( n \atop x \right) p^{x}(1-p)^{n-x}\\ & = & p \sum_{x=1}^{n} x \left( n \atop x \right) p^{x-1}(1-p)^{n-x} \end{eqnarray}

Consider the following polynomial's derivative with respect to \(P\)

\begin{eqnarray} (P + Q)^{n} & = & \sum_{x=0}^{n} \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x}Q^{n-x}\\ \frac{\partial}{\partial P}(P + Q)^{n} & = & n(P + Q)^{n-1} = \sum_{x=0}^{n} x \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x-1}Q^{n-x} \\ & = & \frac{1}{P} \left( \sum_{x=0}^{n} x \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x}Q^{n-x}\right)\\ nP(P + Q)^{n-1} & = & \sum_{x=0}^{n} x \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x}Q^{n-x} \end{eqnarray}

Putting \(P=p\) and \(Q=1-p\), we have

\begin{equation} \mathbb{E}[X] = pn(p+1-p)^{n-1} = pn \end{equation}

The variance

\begin{eqnarray} \text{Var}[X] & = & \mathbb{E}[X^{2}] - \mathbb{E}[X]^{2}\\ & = & \sum_{x=0}^{n} x^{2} \left( n \atop x \right)p^{x}(1-p)^{n-x} - n^{2}p^{2} \end{eqnarray}

To calculate the first term in the last line, let us consider the following second derivative of the polynomial \((P+Q)^{n}\)

\begin{eqnarray} \frac{\partial^{2}}{\partial P^{2}}(P + Q)^{n} & = & n(n-1)(P + Q)^{n-2} \nonumber\\ & = & \sum_{x=2}^{n} x(x-1) \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x-2}Q^{n-x} \nonumber\\ & = & \sum_{x=2}^{n} x^{2} \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x-2}Q^{n-x} - \sum_{x=2}^{n} x \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x-2}Q^{n-x} \nonumber\\ & = & \frac{1}{P^{2}} \left( \sum_{x=2}^{n} x^{2} \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x}Q^{n-x} - \sum_{x=2}^{n} x \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x}Q^{n-x} \right) \nonumber\\ & = & \frac{1}{P^{2}} \left( \sum_{x=1}^{n} x^{2} \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x}Q^{n-x} - \sum_{x=1}^{n} x \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x}Q^{n-x} \right) \nonumber\\ & = & \frac{1}{P^{2}} \left( \sum_{x=1}^{n} x^{2} \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x}Q^{n-x} - nP(P+Q)^{n-1} \right) \nonumber\\ \sum_{x=1}^{n} x^{2} \left( n \atop x \right)P^{x}Q^{n-x} & = & n(n-1)P^{2}(P + Q)^{n-2} + nP(P+Q)^{n-1} \nonumber \end{eqnarray}

Putting \(P=p\) and \(Q=1-p\), we have

\begin{equation} \sum_{x=0}^{n} x^{2} \left( n \atop x \right)p^{x}(1-p)^{n-x} = n(n-1)p^{2} + np \end{equation}

Hence

\begin{equation} \text{Var}[X] = n(n-1)p^{2} + np - n^{2}p^{2} = np(1-p) \end{equation}

The moment generating function

\begin{eqnarray} M(t) = \mathbb{E}[e^{tX}] & = & \sum_{x=0}^{n} e^{tx} \left( n \atop x \right)p^{x}(1-p)^{n-x} \\ & = & \sum_{x=0}^{n} \left( n \atop x \right) (pe^{t})^{x}(1-p)^{n-x} \\ & = & (1-p +pe^{t})^{n} \end{eqnarray}
